MARKS, THIRD ATTEMPT

The pulse read as a spectrum analyser, and it was the bell shape that did it: bars rising to a peak is what sound looks like. These use a baseline instead, which turns the same marks from music into measurement. A scale, a tally, a notch cut into something.
THE SCALE
A ruler, with one division standing taller. The baseline is what stops it being sound: marks hanging off a line are a measurement, and measurement of a span is exactly what the word means.
THE TALLY
Four strokes and the fifth struck through: how anyone alone has ever counted days. Nothing says a world that kept going while you were away more plainly, and no synthesizer has ever looked like it.
THE MISSING RUNG
Four lines, and the third one broken. The interval is the absence, not the marks around it. Reads as a record with something left out, which is closer to the truth than a picture of time ever gets.
THE TURNING
Carried over. A ring with one mark at the top: a world that comes round whether or not anyone is watching, which is the line already printed under your wordmark.
